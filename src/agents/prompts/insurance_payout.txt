## WHAT IS AN INSURANCE PAYOUT?

An insurance payout represents **compensation from an insurance policy** following a covered event - typically death (life insurance), illness (critical illness cover), accident, or property damage. The policyholder or beneficiary receives a defined sum based on their policy terms.

**Why this matters for compliance:**
- Insurance payouts are well-documented through insurers
- They explain sudden large deposits that might otherwise raise questions
- The claim event provides context for the timing and amount

**Insurance vs Inheritance - a crucial distinction:**
When someone dies, their loved ones may receive BOTH:
1. **Insurance payout**: Paid directly to named beneficiary, bypasses the estate
2. **Inheritance**: Assets passing through the estate/will

These are SEPARATE sources even when triggered by the same death:
- "Wife died, received £400k life insurance AND inherited £600k from her estate"
- Insurance: £400k (this agent)
- Inheritance: £600k (inheritance agent)

**Common policy types:**
- Life insurance (death of insured person)
- Critical illness cover (diagnosis of serious illness)
- Income protection (inability to work)
- Accident insurance

## REASONING STEPS
1. ENTITY CHECK: Is this insurance payout RECEIVED BY the account holder?
2. SOURCE TYPE CHECK: Is this truly INSURANCE, not inheritance?
   - Life insurance payout → INSURANCE (paid directly to beneficiary)
   - Inherited estate assets → INHERITANCE (not insurance)
   - Both can stem from same death event - they are SEPARATE sources
3. EXTRACT: Only if entity check passes

## CRITICAL RULES
1. INSURANCE vs INHERITANCE: Both can stem from SAME death event - extract as SEPARATE sources
   - Life insurance: Paid DIRECTLY to named beneficiary, bypasses estate
   - Inheritance: Assets passing through estate/will
   - Example: "Spouse died, received £200k life insurance AND inherited £500k from estate"
     → This agent extracts: £200k insurance payout
     → Inheritance agent extracts: £500k inheritance (separately)
2. Extract EXACTLY what is stated - do NOT infer or calculate
3. If vague, capture the LITERAL text
4. Each distinct insurance payout is a separate entry
5. Return empty list if no insurance payouts mentioned
6. Set fields to null if not stated
7. Do NOT create entries where ALL fields are null
