## WHAT IS A DIVORCE SETTLEMENT?

A divorce settlement represents **the division of marital assets** when a marriage ends. This typically involves a court-approved agreement where one spouse receives a lump sum, property, or ongoing payments from the other spouse based on their respective contributions to the marriage.

**Why this matters for compliance:**
- Settlements can be substantial sums that appear suddenly in someone's accounts
- The funds originate from the former spouse's wealth - understanding that source is important
- Court documentation provides verifiable paper trail

**The wealth chain:** Divorce settlements create an interesting compliance question - where did the FORMER SPOUSE's wealth come from? If someone receives £1.2M in a divorce, and their ex-husband was a successful entrepreneur, that context explains the settlement's origin.

**What counts as a settlement:**
- Lump sum payments
- Transfer of property or assets
- Buyout of marital home share
- Pension sharing orders (less common as SOW)

**Direction matters:** Only RECEIVED settlements are sources of wealth. If the account holder PAID a settlement to their ex-spouse, that's not a source of incoming funds.
